About Us

Hotel le Bonheur has its roots in the beginning of the 20th century when it was a successful boutique hotel with nearly 100% fill-rates year-round. The hotel survived both World Wars and the Great Depression. However, in the 60s, its ultraconservative policies caused a decline in popularity.

During the 70s and early 80s, renamed as Happiness Hotel, it hosted cheerful non-paying residents like Rowlf, Scooter, Swedish Chef, and more — even Kermit and Fozzie Bear made appearances. The fun ended as residents matured and moved on, and the hotel fell into disrepair.

In the late 90s, Ronald Charriott purchased and restored the hotel, reviving its original name. Despite a failed marriage, the hotel thrived again. Now owned by his ex-wife, the hotel demands modern, efficient management — that's where you come in.

Your challenge: grow the business, stay competitive, and make the hotel a premier destination once again.
